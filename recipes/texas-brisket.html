Texas-Style Smoked Brisket

Ingredients

  • 1 whole beef brisket (12-14 lbs)
  • 1/4 cup coarse black pepper
  • 1/4 cup kosher salt
  • 2 tablespoons garlic powder
  • 2 tablespoons onion powder
  • Oak or hickory wood chunks

Instructions

  1. 1.

    Trim the brisket, leaving about 1/4 inch of fat cap. Remove any hard pieces of fat and silver skin from the meat side.

  2. 2.

    Combine pepper, salt, garlic powder, and onion powder. Apply rub generously to all sides of the brisket.

  3. 3.

    Preheat your smoker to 250¬∞F (121¬∞C). Add wood chunks according to manufacturer's instructions.

  4. 4.

    Place brisket fat side up and smoke until internal temperature reaches 165¬∞F (74¬∞C), about 6-8 hours.

  5. 5.

    Wrap brisket tightly in butcher paper and continue smoking until internal temperature reaches 203¬∞F (95¬∞C), about 4-6 more hours.

Pro Tips

  • Let the brisket rest for at least 1 hour before slicing
  • Slice against the grain for maximum tenderness
  • Monitor your fire closely to maintain consistent temperature
